The Plot Thickens

After defeating the blood elemental the guards congratulate you on your victory and begin to assist the citizens injured in the attack. The guard commander ushers you away to the Ducal Palace, where you enter a room with the three agents pursuing the Tome - the chief intelligence agent of Baldur's Gate, Lord Balkal, the First Reader of Candlekeep, Master Galimateas, and Magmoran, the priest of the church of Oghma.

Lord Balkal speaks first, "Thank you for dealing with the situation so expediently. I have gathered all the pursuers of the book here for your convenience. Please make your choice as to which of us shall take possession of this dangerous artifact. I assure you their is no coercion here - Baldur's Gate is a city of free people."

============
Netheril was an empire of sorcerer-kings. It was a nation where magic flourished - indeed, to be a citizen of Netheril was to be a wielder of some arcane magic. Non-magic users were slaves in ancient Netheril.

The nation was composed of various city-states, but these were not merely tribes with a loose alliance. The city states of Netheril were flying fortresses of terrible power. Each city was the domain of a mighty sorcerer - to be the ruler of a city, one had to prove their talent by crafting it.

The greatest of these sorcerers was a young half-elf named Karsus - an arcane prodigy. He was the youngest ever to forge his own city, accomplishing the feat after only 14 years of age. As he grew older, his arcane powers grew, as did his political influence within the Netherese government and his popularity among the peoples of the empire. The Netherese won many wars and acquired a vast territory - eventually only the cities of Neverwinter and Baldur's Gate remained beyond their control.

Then the war came with the Phaerimm. The Phaerimm are not a nation of men or elves, but rather a terrible race of demons who feed on raw, arcane magic. Netheril's flying fortresses were the perfect feeding ground for these beasts. One by one, the fortresses of Netheril were being destroyed.

It was then that Karsus made his desperate attempt to save his empire. Knowing that arcane magic alone could not defeat the Phaerimm, he performed a ritual attempting to usurp the power of the Goddess Mystryl, whose power enabled the use of magic in our world. Karsus did not foresee the magnitude of Mystryl's responsibilities. He took her power but could not regulate the flow of magic in the world, and in that moment, magic faltered. The last thing he saw was the armada of Netheril cities crashing to the ground, as the magic that sustained them had failed.

The Phaerimm, finding themselves stranded and with no source of magic to feed on, were starved to extermination, but the damage was done. Netheril was no more.

At this point, X pauses, looking grimly out the window before he continues.

At least, that's what we thought. We now know that at least one of the Netherese cities escaped into the Shadowfell just before Karsus' Folly. No doubt they found themselves unable to return, as magic had failed all of the world and would not return for a century. In the Shadowfell, the last of the Netherese waited. What they did to survive for centuries in that plane, I do not know, but one thing is for certain - they have returned, and they are not the same people who they once were.

To be sure, the Netherese were always a proud people. Arrogant, proud, and powerful. They conquered because they believed their culture to be superior to all others, not out of desire for more land or wealth. But those that have returned are different. They wrap themselves in shadow and darkness and have a thirst for blood. They have quickly reclaimed most of their ancient lands, but they seem to have little interest in expanding their population. Our intelligence tells us that few survive any Netherese attack. Furthermore, little is known about the new society of Netheril. All we know is that the flying city of Shade is the seat of power, and its occupant is the High Prince of Netheril. It is his agents that hunt you.

As to the nature of the jewel you carry, I can make only one conclusion: it is a shard of Karsus. You found it in the High Forest, where centuries ago several fortresses of Netheril were entombed. You say that the Lady of Spellguard spoke of the Fall of Netheril when she beheld it, but also that it was beyond her power, which is not inconsiderable. You tell me that it appears to have a will of its own, so it must be an artifact beyond the power of a mere enchanted jewel.
This pearl is a fragment of the soul of Karsus - indeed it is said that he was entombed in black stone after his battle with the goddess - and thus it is not only arcane but divine, for it undoubtedly holds a part of Mystryl's essence as well.

The power of a god - even a dead god - is mighty indeed. This is why the ruler of Netheril seeks it. I fear if he obtains it, all the world may fall under his Shadow.

The High Forest

You make your way through the familiar paths of the High Forest and the Dire Wood, following the map on the note to reach the site of the archaeological dig.

Night is beginning to fall when you reach a clearing in the treeline: below you is a large valley ablaze with torchlight. There is a large camp set up and a buzzing of activity as workers go about digging here and there. There appears to be a large tunnel not unlike a mineshaft at the far end of the camp, leading underground somewhere. Most of the people in the camp are humanoid and appear to be slaves - only here and there do you get a glimpse of a shadovar task master driving labor. At the east end of the camp you see a set of large tents much cleaner and well-fashioned than the rest - this must be where Brennus resides.

Positioned at the edges of the camp are sentries - again, most are human but a few Netherese seargants patrol the border as well.

The City of Karse

The city of Karse is vast and littered with the remains of the dead. Here and there the magic of the city remains alive and well - the floating lanterns bask the streets in an eerie blue glow, and water runs endlessly in magical fountains.

You come upon an intersection when you hear a rustling and the distinct sound of a bow flexing. "Who's there? Show yourselves or I'll fire!"

Stepping out from behind a pile of rubble is a middle aged half-elven woman, her hair the gold color autumn leaves. She wears armor that has seen better days, and carries a long sword and a bow. Her cloak bears a pin of silver in the shape of a harp and a crescent moon. "I am Elariel Taias," she begins, "and I did not expect any company down here! I am an explorer of the Crescent Moon Mercantile company."

---

Before you have a chance to answer Elariel, you hear the sound of oncoming footsteps and turn your head to see shadovar warriors sprinting around the corner. The Spire Karsus

You reach a grand plaza in the center of the ghostly city, finding the base of the tower of Karsus. It is immensely tall, overlooking the ruined city center. Outside its gates stands a large statue of marble inlaid with mithril, depicting the dead archmage himself.

"Hurry inside!" beckons Elariel.

--

The interior of the tower can only be described as opulent. It seems hardly touched by the fall of the city. Elariel leads you up floor after floor, guiding you through disabled traps laid by the ancient wizard before arriving at a large door. "I was searching for a key before I found you," she says, producing a crystal keystone not unlike those you found in Spellguard. She places the stone in the door's lock and it creaks open, revealing a large hall that must be the tower's library. There are countless bookshelves filled with ancient tomes. The hall is lighted by arcane candleholders made of the finest mithril.

---
As you do X, the arcane candles suddenly burn red and runes shine on the marble floor, instantly erecting arcane barriers around you. The candleholders begin dispersing a purple gas. "Stranglemist!" cries Elariel. "We have to get out of here, or we'll be dead in 2 minutes!"

Karsus' Folly

"Praise the gods" Elariel mutters, heaving heavily and coughing. "I thought I was doomed for sure."

She looks at you, smiling, and says "That one makes us even, I suppose."

You journey for another 10 minutes upward without event before suddenly emerging onto the grand plateau atop the tower. The view is breathtaking - all of the city is under your purview.

You are not caught up with the view for long, however, as the center of the plateau holds a far more interesting object: an statue of blackest marble depicting an archmage clawing at the heavens, intense pain wracked across his features. Here and there is an imperfection in the statue, where a piece of it was chipped away.

You begin to approach the statue when it alarmingly seems to look at you - this statue is alive! "By the gods," exclaims Elariel, "it is Karsus himself, encased in a prison of stone."

The air shatters around you with a thunderous voice "Who dares disturb my rest!" The statue's eyes begin to bleed.

"I am Lord Karsus, ruler of Netheril and conquerer of Mystryl. I am a man and yet more than a man. For two thousand years I have slumbered in my dream and yet now I awake. Why is this so? Did I not seal my fate when I killed the goddess?" the voice muses, seeming to forget that you are there.

"Unless...unless part of me was not lost in that battle. And now that fragment of my soul has so graciously been returned to me."
At the embodied voice's words the Pearl tears itself from Ammon's neck and embeds itself upon the stone crown of the statue, filling in a small hole in his brow. "I see...I see. Part of me was separated, and how it has labored to bring me back! It has played this group of adventurers for fools. But you are not the only players. Lord Shadow escaped my folly and even now seeks to return Netheril to glory from the Shadowfell."

"I must regenerate. I must gather all the remaining pieces of my soul. It will take some time, but now my will has returned."

Finally remembering that you are before him, the voice says "I thank you for your service. I take no pleasure in your deaths. I cannot yet take form, but the barest inkling of my power returns to me and I must use it to dispatch you."

The air hums with intensity as the statue suddenly becomes encased in adamantine stone. A vicious roar fills the air around you. "DRAGON!" shouts Elariel.

Sweeping on the air towards you is a dragon of mithril!

Xanros Arrives

The dragon gives a shout and collapses in death.

"Amusing. You are stronger than I sense. It is no matter. My old friends are coming to destroy you."

Bursting through the tower door is Xanros and his soldiers. He grins and then an expression of awe comes over him as he notices Karsus. "By the Raven Queen!" he exclaims, "Can it be? Have you led us right to our goal?"

"Yes, they have indeed, Tulien Xanros, first servant of the High Prince Tanthul Telamont, Lord Shadow, ruler of the city of Shade, last of the Netherese. I am Karsus. Our people are saved. Dispatch these heroes."

Xanros springs to his feet, but Elariel is quicker, sending a shadowbane arrow flying at Xanros. It misses and hits a soldier, who falls to his knees in pain, his body consumed by the flame.

"Time for the backup plan!" she shouts, pulling out a scroll and beginning to mutter words in some lost language. A portal tears itself open in the air just off the edge of the plateau. "We've got to jump for it!" she screams, turning and running.

Athletics DC 15 to make the portal. If you fail, the Netherese warriors catch you with an arrow, dealing 3d6 damage.
